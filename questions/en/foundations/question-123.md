---
question: "What is the main difference between an organization member and an outside collaborator for a repository owned by that organization?"
documentation: "https://docs.github.com/en/organizations/managing-access-to-your-organizations-repositories/adding-outside-collaborators-to-repositories-in-your-organization"
---

- [x] Outside collaborators have repository-level access but are not part of the organization; members belong to the organization and may have broader access and roles.  
- [ ] Outside collaborators can manage billing settings for the organization and view financial information, while members cannot access these settings.  
- [ ] Members can never contribute to private repositories in the organization regardless of permissions, while outside collaborators can always contribute.  
- [ ] Outside collaborators automatically become team maintainers in the organization and can manage team membership and repository access.
