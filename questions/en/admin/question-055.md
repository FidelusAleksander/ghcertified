---
question: "What distinguishes a visible team from a secret team in a GitHub organization?"
documentation: "https://docs.github.com/en/organizations/organizing-members-into-teams/about-teams"
---

- [x] Visible teams can be viewed and mentioned by any organization member, whereas secret teams are only visible to team members and organization owners, enhancing privacy for sensitive collaborations.
- [ ] Visible teams have access to all repositories by default, while secret teams do not have any repository access until explicitly granted.
- [ ] Secret teams can manage organization settings, unlike visible teams which can only manage repository settings.
- [ ] There is no functional difference; the terms simply reflect the team's preference for communication.
